Tuberculosis (TB) is a potentially serious infectious disease that mainly affects your lungs. The bacteria that cause tuberculosis are spread from one person to another through tiny droplets released into the air via coughs and sneezes.

In the past 3 months, there has been 32 diagnosed cases of tuberculosis.

The average cost to treat a case of tuberculosis is P650.


Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne tropical disease caused by the dengue virus. Symptoms typically begin three to fourteen days after infection. This may include a high fever, headache, vomiting, muscle and joint pains, and a characteristic skin rash.

In the past 3 months, there has been 128 diagnosed cases of dengue.

The average cost to treat a case of dengue is P1,130.



Polio, short for poliomyelitis, or infantile paralysis, is an infectious disease caused by the poliovirus. In about 0.5 percent of cases there is muscle weakness resulting in an inability to move.

In the past 3 months, there has been 2 diagnosed cases of polio.

The average cost to treat a case of polio is P350.


Scabies is one of several skin conditions that can cause itching and rashes. It is a dermatologic condition caused by sarcoptes scabiei, an eight-legged microscopic mite. Scabies is contagious and can spread very easily from person to person through close physical contact.

In the past 3 months, there has been 571 diagnosed cases of scabies.

The average cost to treat a case of scabies is P150.
